Our Story

Est. 2020 in La Vergne, TN

Cimorene’s Jubilee is Gwen Etges, Kyle Etges, and their dog Cimorene!

Since the Spring of 2020, Gwen has been making homemade jams, butters, and sauces using fresh, local ingredients. She wanted to make jam for her husband Kyle, who’s favorite food is a good-old fashioned peanut butter and jelly sandwich. Kyle soon had more jam than he could possibly eat, so they began selling at the local La Vergne farmer’s market. Even Cim comes to help out! Since then, they have sold at the La Vergne Old Timer’s Festival, and have big plans for the new year! You can purchase jam from Gwen anytime during the offseason right here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Commonly asked jam-related questions

  • Does your jam have seeds?

    Yes! As of right now I do not have the ability to remove seed from my jams. People seem to like the seeds better as well

  • Do you make sugar free/low sugar jams?

    Yes! But not many. The amount of sugar free or low sugar jam that is made from a batch is much lower than the amount from a regular sugar batch. Because of this I generally have fewer types and jars of the sugar free/low sugar jams.

  • Do you use fresh ingredients?

    I use fresh fruit where I can. But freezing the fruit can bring out a better taste in some fruits so sometimes I freeze them. Some jellies are made from all natural juices and I use the freshest ingredients I can.

  • Why don't you have X-flavored jam?

    If the fruit is in season, I can fulfill any reasonable request. Let me know what you're thinking and we'll make it happen!
